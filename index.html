Royal geranium or pelargonium blooms unusually beautifully and fully justifies the word "royal" in its name. Large flowers with wavy edges in bright colors attract her among other flowering plants and attract the eye. Beautiful and its jagged rough leaves.
Pelargonium grows in South America. It was brought to Europe in the 18th century, and since then many new varieties have been bred by breeders.
Over the past few decades, many varieties of this flower have been bred, which have a different shape and bloom with multi-colored flowers. There are also varieties with variegated, pale red and white-pink petals. Everyone will be able to choose for themselves a shade to their own taste. Flowers often have a double shape, and the petals themselves are corrugated, and sometimes wavy. The plant has large rough leaves with notches. But some newly bred varieties have small leaves and flowers.
